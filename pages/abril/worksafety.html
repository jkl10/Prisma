GREEN APRIL – HEALTH AND SAFETY AWARENESS AT WORK

What it is

The Green April campaign aims to raise awareness about the prevention of work-related accidents and illnesses. The month was chosen because of April 28th, the International Day of Mourning for Workers Injured or Killed on the Job.

Challenges

One of the biggest challenges are work accidents and occupational diseases, such as:

  • Carpal Tunnel Syndrome, tendinitis, bursitis, lower back pain, among others;
  • Musculoskeletal disorders (conditions that affect the musculoskeletal system);
  • Respiratory diseases (such as asthma and bronchitis);
  • Dermatitis;
  • Hearing loss (noise-induced);
  • Cardiovascular diseases;
  • Occupational cancers (a type of cancer caused by exposure to carcinogens in the work environment);

Leave of Absence due to Mental Disorders

In 2024, according to the Ministry of Social Security (MPS), Brazil recorded 472,328 leaves of absence due to mental disorders, such as anxiety and depression. This data indicates a worrying increase of 68% compared to the previous year, reflecting the growing concern about mental health in the work environment.

Culture of Accident Prevention

Companies play a fundamental role in protecting the health of their workers, not only by complying with safety standards but also by creating an environment where prevention is a daily priority. In the activities carried out by the institution, it is emphasized that the practice of accident prevention involves promoting continuous training, providing adequate protective equipment, and conducting management to identify and correct potential risks. This makes it possible to ensure a safe work environment.

Where to Report

If your workplace is putting you or your colleagues at risk, contact the Ministry of Labor and report it.
