May FURTA-COR - raising awareness of maternal mental health.

What it is

May "Furta-Cor" is a campaign for maternal mental health, being the largest in Latin America. The month of May was chosen because it is when Mother's Day is celebrated. "Furta-Cor" is a color whose hue changes according to the light it receives, having no single, absolute color. In the spectrum of motherhood, it is no different; it encompasses all colors.

The campaign's objective is to sensitize, alert, raise awareness, promote, and stimulate society to face this problem head-on, opening a broader space for discussion about how to help this very important part of the population.

Its Importance

We are increasingly witnessing diseases like depression and anxiety expanding worldwide, and this also affects maternal figures. There is a strong social stigma surrounding topics related to mental health, and when it extends to the maternal field, this stigma is even more reinforced.

Little or no attention has been given to the factors contributing to the mental suffering of women facing the increasing demands of motherhood. This campaign seeks to bring light to all those who have been suffering in the dark.

How to Participate

May "Furta-Cor" is a collective movement, and every contribution makes a difference. Individuals, institutions, and companies can engage in various ways:

  • Promoting knowledge: lectures, conversation circles, walks, and cultural events.
  • Enabling actions: with material, financial, or logistical support.
  • Amplifying the voice:sharing our content and campaigns on social media.
  • Engaging actively:participating in free actions and mobilizations throughout the month of May.

Professional and Public Health Support:

  • Public Health Network: – Basic health units and Psychosocial Care Centers (CAPS) offer mental health care and support for mothers in various municipalities and in the Federal District.
  • Psychology and Psychiatry Professionals:– Seeking professional help is essential. Many regional psychology councils and other entities offer guidance on how to find specialized support.
