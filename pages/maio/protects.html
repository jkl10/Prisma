ORANGE MAY –CHILD PROTECTION AWARENESS

What it is

The Orange May campaign aims to raise awareness about the figh against sexual abuse and exploitation of children and adolescents. The month of May was chosen because of May 18th, the National Day for Combating these Crimes.

Campaign Objective

This campaign seeks to give visibility to this cause and promote child protection. It involves awareness-raising actions in schools, communities, and public spaces.

The numbers demonstrate the seriousness of the problem:

  • Every hour, 3 children are abused in Brazil.
  • About 51% are between 1 and 5 years old.
  • Todos os anos, 500 mil crianças e adolescentes são explorados sexualmente no país.
  • Every year, 500,000 children and adolescents are sexually exploited in our country, and data suggests that only 7.5% of cases are reported to the authorities, meaning the actual numbers are much higher.

Protecting children is everyone's responsibility - family, school, State and society.

Project MAPEAR

MAPEAR is an initiative by the Federal Highway Police (PRF), in partnership with Childhood Brazil, which identifies locations vulnerable to the sexual exploitation of children and adolescents (ESCA) on federal highways in Brazil. Between 2023 and 2024, in its 10th Edition, 17,687 spots were mapped.

The mapping supports operations that promote a significant reduction in the criticality of these areas. The project is structured on the joint action of public, private, and civil society agencies aiming to prevent ESCA and protect victims.

Results of the Latest Mapping

In the 2023/2024 biennium, 17,687 spots were mapped across the country, an increase of 83.2% compared to the previous edition.

  • 807 spots were classified as critical (4.6%)
  • 2,566 as high risk (14.5%)
  • 5,237 as medium risk (29.6%)
  • 9,077 as low risk (51.3%)

Despite the large increase in the number of vulnerable spots, there was a substantial decrease in the number of critical and high-risk spots.

Por que falar sobre isso?

Child abuse most oftn occurs within the family environment or close to it, which makes identification and reporting difficult. Silence is the abuser's greatest ally, and awareness is the main protective tool.

Society must learn to recognize signs, know how to act, who to call, and understand that any suspicion must be reported.

Protecting a child is never an exaggeration-it is a moral, social, and legal obligation.

Warning signs that cannot be ignored.

  • Children who are victims of abuse may exhibit:
  • Sudden chages in behavior.
  • Excessive fear of people or environments.
  • Behavioral regression (such as bedwetting again).
  • Sexualized drawings or language.
  • Agressiveness, constant sadness, or isolation.
  • Unexplained pain,injuries, or physical discomfort.

Early detection can save a life.
